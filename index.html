Motivation

  • Compared with architectural projects, which take years to decades to complete, front-end development projects take considerably shorter time to deliver. In addition to shorter project cycles,I find the main attraction of front-end design lies in intensive communication with various users, which entails responding to their feedback swiftly and flexibly. Effective conversation with users, in my opinion, is of great significance for a designer. While undergoing training in front-end design, I realize I will be working in a wide-ranging business and have to harness a set of fast-evolving skills, but for me challenges from emerging areas are always welcome.

    Today knowledge is always interdisciplinary, and I always seek to apply and integrate what I have learned from the diverse areas in my career. My training in mathematics in university allowed me to specialize in digital design in architecture. Meanwhile, working in architectural design has equipped me with the ability to manage and organize a project as well as my aesthetic sensibility, both of which are requirements of becoming a front-end designer.
